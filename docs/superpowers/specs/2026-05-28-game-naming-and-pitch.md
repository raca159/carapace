# Game Naming & Elevator Pitch

> **Date:** 2026-05-28
> **Status:** Finalized

---

## Game Title

**Carapace: The Relict**

### Alternatives Considered

| Name | Angle | Why it lost |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Carapace (standalone) | Original working title | Good but wanted to explore alternatives |
| Instar | Biological stage between molts | Strong but too obscure for general audience |
| Chela | Crustacean claw | Too obscure |
| Molt | Shedding/transformation | Good but less distinctive |
| Phage | Devouring/consumption | Aggressive but narrow |
| Relict (standalone) | Ancient survivor | Strong but loses the Carapace brand |
| Voidshell | Dark + structural | Good but fabricated compound |
| Phage Instar | Two-word biological | Too narrow/specific |
| Reliquary | Container for relics | Elegant but doesn't evoke the game |
| The Relict Carapace | Full compound | Too long, reads like a lore item |

### Decision

"Carapace" retains the original brand identity, the biological shell imagery, and the connection to the Great Carapace lore. "The Relict" adds the survivor/Remnant concept — a surviving organism from an earlier era — and gives the subtitle a dark, haunting weight. Together they communicate biological horror, ancient survival, and transformation.

The title tested well in blind pitch: readers immediately associated it with grimdark sci-fi, post-apocalyptic isolation, biological armor, and uncovering forgotten history — all core to the game.

## Elevator Pitch

Wake into a deeply simulated roguelike where immortal crustaceans breed in the trenches below, their blood-thirsty offspring infiltrate the courts above, and familiar cultists rule the ruins of a collapsed civilization. Telomerase enzyme grants immortality, and has poisoned everything it touches. Harvest it from the dead and trade in blood to survive a feudal world built on forgotten technology, where every transaction costs a piece of your humanity. A procedurally generated sandbox full of emergent stories, biological economies, and worlds that generate their own history with or without you.

### Usage

- Steam page short description
- Landing page hero text
- Social media / press kit summary
- Any context where the game needs to be explained in under 15 seconds
